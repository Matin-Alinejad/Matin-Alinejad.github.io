About Me

I am a final-year Electrical Engineering student at Sharif University of Technology, driven by a profound passion for the intersection of causality, machine learning, and control systems. My research focuses on understanding and enhancing the causal reasoning capabilities of AI systems, particularly Large Language Models.

My work involves developing novel frameworks and benchmarks to test and improve how models understand cause-and-effect relationships. I am committed to bridging the gap between statistical machine learning and true causal understanding, with applications ranging from AI safety to robust decision-making in complex environments.

Currently, I lead research initiatives at RIML-Lab and collaborate with international researchers to advance the field of causal AI.
